## Problem

Token-sale products often optimise for on-chain mechanics and neglect admin operations, payment reconciliation, and user support. This platform needed both sale-contract logic and an operator layer that could handle real customer and finance workflows.

## Approach

Design the system around two realities: the token-sale contracts must be deterministic and audit-friendly, while the business layer must support manual review, payment handling, and campaign control.

## Architecture

- Solidity sale contracts handling allocation and distribution logic
- Admin frontend for campaign configuration, investor monitoring, and exception handling
- Node.js backend bridging contract events, Stripe flows, and internal reporting
- Reconciliation routines for on-chain and off-chain payment states

## Outcome

- Operators could manage sale configuration without touching contracts directly
- Payment exceptions became observable instead of buried in support tickets
- Finance and product teams had a shared operational view of sale status

## Lessons

The hard part of launch platforms is not contract deployment. It is making the business process resilient when users pay through multiple rails and expect immediate clarity.
