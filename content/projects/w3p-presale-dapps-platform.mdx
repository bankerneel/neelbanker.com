## Problem

Presale ecosystems look simple from the outside, but in practice they combine campaign logic, wallet-linked frontends, admin operations, user support concerns, and multiple payment or transaction states that need to reconcile cleanly.

## Approach

Design the platform as an operational system rather than a landing page with wallet connect. The DApps layer handled user access and interaction, while backend services handled campaign control, reporting, and consistency across business workflows.

## Architecture

- Angular DApps for user-facing access to sale and product flows
- Backend services for CRM operations, campaign logic, and reporting
- Shared wallet-linked state across frontend and backend touchpoints
- Operational flows built to tolerate asynchronous confirmation and business-side exceptions

## Outcome

- Product and operations teams had better visibility into live sale flows
- User-facing experiences stayed cleaner because support-heavy logic was moved out of the frontend
- Delivery became easier to coordinate across technical and non-technical stakeholders

## Lessons

Presale platforms fail when they are designed as marketing experiences first and operational systems second. The business logic is the product, not just the front-end funnel.
