## Design Context

### Users
Companies, startups, and individuals at any stage who need senior technical judgment — founders evaluating architecture decisions, CTOs looking for a trusted advisor, engineering leaders who need someone to step in when complexity is already high. They arrive with a specific problem, often mid-crisis. They're evaluating quickly: can this person actually help us?

### Brand Personality
**Three words: precise, authoritative, sharp.**

Not warm, not playful — this is the best person in the room. The interface should feel like a proprietary terminal crossed with a premium editorial publication. Like a Bloomberg screen run by someone who also cares about craft. Confidence without spectacle.

Initial inspiration: landonorris.com — kinetic, dark, premium, bold typography, nothing generic about it. The Lando reference is about feeling like the best at what you do, not about being flashy.

### Emotional Goals
Every visitor should leave feeling:
- "This person is the best in the room"
- "I can trust them to fix our mess"
- "I want to hire them immediately"

No hedging. No "available for projects." Maximum authority, minimum noise.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark-only. Near-black editorial (`hsl(0 0% 3.5%)`), warm foreground (`hsl(36 15% 91%)`).
- **Palette**: Lime primary (`hsl(72 100% 49%)`), cyan AI accent (`hsl(190 100% 52%)`), orange leadership (`hsl(25 100% 60%)`). Use sparingly — lime is the single dominant accent, the others only in pillar context.
- **Typography**: Barlow Condensed for all display/UI (replaces Syne), Azeret Mono for labels/metrics/code (replaces JetBrains Mono). Compressed, industrial, earned — not generic tech.
- **Layout**: `gap-px bg-border` grid compositing is the site's signature visual move. Preserve it everywhere.
- **Motion**: Motion-rich hero is intentional. Interior pages are restrained. Keep that contrast.
- **No**: Gradient text, side-stripe borders, glassmorphism cards, identical card grids, hero metric layouts.

### Typography (locked)
Syne + JetBrains Mono. These were a deliberate choice — Syne's wide letterforms give "NEEL BANKER" and display headings a logo-like presence that condensed alternatives don't replicate. Do not replace. The impeccable reflex-reject flag is noted but overridden by user intent.

### Design Principles
1. **Authority over explanation.** Copy should state facts, not pitch. Numbers speak, adjectives don't.
2. **The grid is the brand.** The `gap-px bg-border` tessellating grid system defines the site's visual fingerprint. Never replace it with cards or shadows.
3. **Lime is rare, which is why it works.** Primary accent at 10% visual weight. Overuse kills it.
4. **Conversion before decoration.** Every page needs a visible path to contact or booking. CTAs don't hide.
5. **Sharp edges, no apology.** Zero border-radius on interactive elements. The aesthetic is editorial, not consumer SaaS.

### Banned Patterns (enforced Apr 2026)
- `background-clip: text` gradient fills — absolute ban, removes AI tell from hero eyebrow
- Absolutely-positioned `w-[3px]` stripe divs on cards/list items — removes side-stripe anti-pattern
- `border-l border-primary` as decorative accent — same pattern, different implementation
- `transition-all` in custom components — use `transition-colors` only
- Service cards or section CTAs with no actionable link — every high-intent section needs a direct path to booking
